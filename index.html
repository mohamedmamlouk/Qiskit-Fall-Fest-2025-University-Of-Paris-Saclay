About the Fall Fest

This year, a Qiskit Fall Fest event will be hosted by Université Paris-Saclay. Join us from November 17–21, 2025, for an immersive week in the world of quantum computing. Whether you're a beginner or an expert, this event offers something for everyone. Experience a diverse program of hands-on workshops, quantum programming sessions, and hackathons, alongside industry meetups, lab visits, and panel discussions with experts from academia and IBM Quantum. You’ll also find opportunities for career guidance, networking, and practical training designed to bridge the gap between learning and innovation. Registration is free and open to all students and enthusiasts eager to explore the quantum frontier — come be part of the global Qiskit community and help shape the future of quantum computing

Our Partners & Sponsors

Qiskit Community

Qiskit is a global ecosystem. It is an open-source initiative supported by learners, developers, educators, and researchers around the world, the Qiskit Community builds resources, tutorials, challenges, and tools that make quantum computing accessible for everyone. Together, we share knowledge, mentor newcomers, celebrate contributions, and push forward on both practical and theoretical fronts. Whether you're writing circuits, experimenting with algorithms, or contributing to documentation, the community welcomes you to be part of the journey.

IBM Quantum

IBM Quantum is at the forefront of building the infrastructure needed for the quantum revolution. From powerful quantum processors to the Qiskit software stack, IBM empowers users—whether in academia, industry, or independent research—to explore, learn, and deploy quantum applications. With ambitious roadmaps toward fault-tolerant quantum systems, scalable hardware, and ever-improving software performance, IBM is committed to making quantum computing a usable tool for solving real-world problems.

Université Paris-Saclay

Université Paris-Saclay is a central pillar in France’s quantum science ecosystem. Through Quantum-Saclay, the university brings together physicists, mathematicians, engineers, computer scientists and more in a collaborative space committed to both foundational theory and cutting-edge quantum technologies. With over forty research groups internationally recognized in areas such as quantum simulation, photonics, sensor technologies, and error correction, Paris-Saclay not only trains the next generation of quantum researchers but also fosters academic-industrial partnerships to turn quantum ideas into impactful innovations.
